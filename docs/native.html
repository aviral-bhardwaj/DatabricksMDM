C · What "Databricks-Native" Really Requires

Native is an architectural property, not a logo on a slide

Three different products can all claim "MDM on Databricks" and only one passes the test. The test is simple: remove every proprietary, non-lakehouse component and ask whether the product still produces and governs a golden record. If it can't, it was integration, not nativeness.

The jump from Tier 3 to Tier 4 is the entire product thesis: delete the hub.

Why most "MDM on Databricks" is still not native

Three failure shapes dominate, and the codebase this product replaces exhibited the third.

  1. The connector illusion (Tier 3). An external hub gains a Delta connector and the deck says "Databricks-native." But the master, the matching, the trust model, and the stewardship all execute in the hub. Databricks is a landing zone. Delete Databricks and the product is unharmed — the definition of not native.
  2. The lift-and-shift (Tier 1.5). A vendor containerizes its app server and runs it next to a workspace. Same monolith, new address. None of Delta's time travel, UC's lineage, or Spark's scale is on the critical path.
  3. The transplant (the trap this blueprint exits). Hand-written PySpark notebooks that re-implement classic MDM imperatively: a EntityMatcher class with a hardcoded Random Forest, a GoldenRecordBuilder with branching survivorship, a bolt-on FastAPI as the only serving path, and README-only governance. It runs on Databricks but it ignores everything that makes Databricks Databricks. It would port to Snowflake by changing a connection string. That portability is the proof of non-nativeness.
Anti-pattern catalogue · the "not truly native" tells

If you see these, you have a Tier 1–3 product wearing a lakehouse costume:

  • JDBC-in / JDBC-out as the spine. Sources read over JDBC, masters written over JDBC, Delta only as scratch. The lakehouse is bypassed.
  • An external match-engine round-trip. Spark ships candidate pairs to a proprietary matcher and waits. Latency, cost, and lock-in, with no in-place explainability.
  • The golden record outside Unity Catalog. The most governed asset in the company lives where UC can't apply row filters, column masks, lineage, or tags.
  • A re-implemented security model. The app has its own users, roles, and ACLs that shadow UC. Two sources of truth for "who can see this PII."
  • A REST API as the only way to read the master. BI, notebooks, and models can't query it without going through an app tier. Data proximity destroyed.
  • Imperative notebooks instead of declarative pipelines. No DLT/Lakeflow expectations, no managed lineage, no incremental semantics — just procedural Spark and cron.
  • No use of system tables. Audit, lineage, billing, and quality are hand-rolled instead of read from system.access, system.lineage, system.billing.
  • Hardcoded ML on the critical path. A model baked into match code, untracked in MLflow, unservable, unversioned, un-A/B-testable.
  • Cloud-portable by accident. Swap the storage layer and nothing breaks — because nothing depended on Delta/UC semantics.

The litmus test

Pull the plug on every non-Databricks component. A native MDM still ingests, standardizes, matches, masters, governs, and serves — because all of that is Delta, UC, Lakeflow, and MLflow. A Tier 1–3 product goes dark, because its brain was always somewhere else.

What legitimately lives outside — and why that's still native

Native does not mean "everything inside Databricks." It means "the data, governance, processing, and intelligence are the lakehouse." Two things correctly sit outside, and pretending otherwise produces a worse product:

  • outside The stewardship UI. Databricks is a data-and-compute plane, not an OLTP application server for thousands of concurrent steward clicks, optimistic locking, and rich form UX. The state (cases, decisions, assignments) lives in Delta; the interface is a real web app reading/writing those tables. The audit trail is the data — fully native — while the pixels are not. Section G defends this in depth.
  • outside High-fanout synchronous APIs. If an operational system needs sub-10ms single-record lookups at high QPS, front the Gold/Master tables with an online store or Lakebase/serving layer — fed from the lakehouse, not a separate source of truth.

Everything else — and especially the golden record itself — belongs inside.
